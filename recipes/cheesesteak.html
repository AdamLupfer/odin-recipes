Philly Cheesesteak

This is a simple recipe for a cheesteak sub sandwich, loosely copying the Jersey Mike's famous philly cheesesteak. I like to add jalapenos and make my own chipotle mayo for a spread, but you can customize this recipe to suit your cheesesteak needs as you deem fit.

To start, all you need is a large pan, but a flattop griddle surface works best for this recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 serving of frozen shaved beef steak planks
  • 1/4 sweet onion, julienned
  • 1/2 cup sweet peppers, sliced lengthwise (jarred works fine)
  • 2 slices of white american cheese
  • salt and pepper
  • A sub sandwich roll

To start, heat the cooking surface to medium high heat

  1. Once the surface is sufficiently heated, add the steak planks and season with salt
  2. Stack the onion and peppers on top of the beef plank
  3. When the beef is brown underneath, flip the beef with a spatula, putting the peppers and onions underneath the beef. Season the beef with more salt
  4. Start chopping the beef and pepper/onion mixture on the cooktop, until finely chopped and mixed
  5. Once the beef is no longer red but still contains a bit of pink, shape the mixture into a portion approximetaly the same length as your sub roll. Stack the 2 slices of american cheese on the mixture and let melt
  6. While the cheese is melting, slice your sub roll lengthwise but only enough to open the bread roll up
  7. Microwave the bread roll for ~20 seconds
  8. When the roll is ready, take it out, holding it in one hand, and scoop the hot beef into the roll, filling it entirely lengthwise

And serve! Serves ~1-2.
